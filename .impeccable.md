# MusicParticle Design Context

## Target Audience
Music lovers who want a visual companion to their listening experience. Concert-goers, DJs, house party hosts, people who play music while working/relaxing. Age 18-45, digitally fluent, appreciate aesthetic experiences. They discover this through sharing: someone sends them the link and says "play music and open this."

## Use Cases
1. Personal listening enhancement: playing music at home and watching particles react
2. Social/party visuals: projecting on a TV/screen during gatherings
3. Concert/DJ visuals: professional-grade reactive visuals for live events
4. Sharing: sending the link to friends as a "you have to see this" moment

## Brand Personality
- **Not a software product.** This is an experience, a sensory amplifier.
- **Tone**: Confident, minimal, slightly mysterious. Like a gallery opening for sound.
- **Feel**: The landing page should feel like the first 10 seconds of walking into an immersive art installation. Quiet anticipation, then sensory reward.
- **Voice**: Short declarative sentences. No exclamation marks. Let the visuals do the talking.

## Design Direction
- **Aesthetic**: Editorial/gallery. Think Mutek festival websites, Boiler Room, Ableton.com. NOT SaaS template.
- **Dark theme required** (#06060c base). This is non-negotiable because the product itself is dark.
- **Typography**: One distinctive display font, one clean body font. NOT the usual suspects.
- **The product IS the hero.** The Three.js visualizer should dominate. Text should be sparse and precise.
- **No cards.** No grids of identical containers. Use editorial layouts with varied rhythm.
